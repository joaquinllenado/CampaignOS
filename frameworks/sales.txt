Sales Intelligence Framework (V1 — High Signal / Low Noise)
Objective
The Sales framework evaluates:
how effectively a campaign generated commercial outcomes


whether audience attention translated into purchase behavior


whether creators drove efficient conversion


whether the campaign generated scalable revenue impact


whether sales performance was sustainable and efficient


The framework is designed to:
move beyond vanity engagement metrics


separate visibility from conversion effectiveness


prioritize efficiency and purchase intent


provide strategic interpretation instead of analytics overload


This framework should feel like:
commercial intelligence,
not:
sales reporting.

Core Principles
1. Sales ≠ Awareness
Sales campaigns are evaluated on:
conversion efficiency


purchase behavior


GMV contribution


creator sales effectiveness


commercial scalability


NOT:
raw views


impressions


passive engagement


A campaign can have:
high visibility


strong engagement


weak conversion


and still underperform on sales objectives.

2. Revenue Efficiency Matters More Than Raw GMV
The framework prioritizes:
efficient conversion


scalable revenue generation


creator sales quality


purchase intent alignment


over:
inflated traffic


passive engagement


isolated revenue spikes



3. Benchmarking Is Contextual
Campaigns should be compared against:
similar sales-focused campaigns


similar creator tiers


similar product categories


similar campaign structures


the brand’s historical sales performance


NOT against:
universal sales benchmarks



4. The AI Interprets Commercial Behavior
The system should explain:
what drove purchases


why audiences converted


which creators influenced sales


where conversion friction existed


what limited revenue efficiency


The user should NOT need to interpret:
GMV formulas


benchmark calculations


conversion mechanics


weighted scoring logic


Those remain internal.

Benchmark Layers
The framework compares campaigns against multiple benchmark contexts.

Platform Benchmark
“How did this perform compared to overall sales-focused campaigns?”

Tier Benchmark
“How did this perform compared to campaigns with similar creator scale and commercial structure?”

Brand Benchmark
“How did this perform compared to the brand’s historical sales campaigns?”

Cohort Benchmark (Future Layer)
“How did this perform compared to campaigns using similar creator, product, and content strategies?”

Campaign Data vs Benchmark Data
Always separate:
What happened
from:
What it means relative to benchmarks
Example:
Campaign Performance
“The campaign generated strong purchase activity across multiple creator segments.”
Benchmark Context
“Conversion efficiency exceeded similar sales-focused campaigns, particularly among tutorial-driven creators.”

Sample Output Template
Sales — Strong
So What?
This campaign converted audience attention into purchases more effectively than similar sales-focused campaigns, particularly through creators with strong product education and audience trust.
Revenue performance was driven by efficient creator conversion rather than broad visibility alone, suggesting strong purchase intent and healthy creator-product alignment.

What Happened
The campaign generated steady purchase activity throughout the campaign lifecycle, with tutorial and review-style creators producing the strongest commercial performance.
Sales remained distributed across multiple creators rather than relying on a single breakout revenue driver.

Compared To Similar Campaigns
Compared to similar sales campaigns:
conversion efficiency was stronger than expected


creator-driven revenue impact was healthier than comparable campaigns


purchase behavior remained more stable over time


creator efficiency exceeded the brand’s historical sales performance


Compared to the brand’s previous campaigns:
creator conversion improved significantly


GMV efficiency increased


sales consistency remained healthier throughout the campaign lifecycle



What Worked Best
Tutorial-style creators generated the strongest purchase behavior.


Product demonstration content produced healthy conversion efficiency.


Mid-sized creators delivered stronger revenue efficiency than larger creators.


Creator consistency helped sustain stable commercial performance throughout the campaign.



What Limited Performance
Several high-visibility creators generated weak purchase conversion relative to engagement.


Revenue performance remained concentrated among a small number of creators.


Some creator segments generated attention without strong buyer intent.



What To Change Right Now
Increase allocation toward tutorial and product-demonstration creators.


Prioritize creators with stronger historical conversion efficiency.


Reduce reliance on creators generating passive engagement without purchases.


Expand creator diversity to reduce revenue concentration risk.


Improve CTA clarity and product education in underperforming content segments.
